FAQs

What is an ad exchange?
An ad exchange is a technology platform that facilitates real-time buying and selling of digital advertising inventory through programmatic auctions. Exchanges connect publishers (supply) with advertisers (demand) using protocols like OpenRTB.
How does an OpenRTB auction work?
When a user visits a page, the exchange constructs a bid request describing the ad opportunity and sends it to multiple DSPs. Each DSP responds with a bid price and creative. The exchange runs an auction to select the winner, typically in under 200 milliseconds.
What is the difference between first-price and second-price auctions?
In a first-price auction, the winner pays exactly what they bid. In a second-price auction, the winner pays one cent above the second-highest bid. Most exchanges have moved to first-price auctions for transparency.
