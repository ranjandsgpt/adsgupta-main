Supply Path Optimization (SPO) Strategy | Ranjan Dasgupta

Ranjan Dasgupta designs SPO strategies that reduce bid duplication and improve auction efficiency. For more, see Insights on programmatic advertising and AdTech.

Why SPO Matters Now

Buyers today are overwhelmed by the number of paths into the same inventory. Without clear SPO strategies, they face higher fees, more noise, and greater operational complexity. At the same time, publishers risk cannibalizing their own revenue by allowing redundant or low-quality paths to proliferate.

Ranjan Dasgupta works with both sides of the marketplace to design SPO strategies that make sense. For buyers, that might mean rationalizing partner lists and preferring paths with strong transparency and performance. For publishers and exchanges, it can mean simplifying how inventory is exposed while still supporting programmatic advertising innovation.

By treating SPO as a product and relationship problem, not just a spreadsheet exercise, Ranjan helps teams focus on durable, trust-building changes that buyers can rely on over time.

Approach to SPO Strategy

Mapping and Classifying Supply Paths

Ranjan begins with a comprehensive map of how inventory flows from publisher to buyer. This includes direct deals, SSP hops, and any resellers identified via ads.txt/app-ads.txt and sellers.json. Once paths are visible, they can be classified by value, risk, and performance.

Aligning with Buyer Preferences

Modern buyers prefer direct and efficient paths. Ranjan works with sales and product teams to understand how major DSPs and trading desks are applying SPO, then adjusts supply representations so that preferred paths align with how buyers are actually bidding.

Reducing Duplication and Preserving Reach

SPO is not about eliminating all intermediaries; it is about keeping the ones that add real value. Ranjan helps maintain reach and demand diversity while pruning duplicative or low-value paths that confuse auctions and erode trust.

Connecting SPO with Yield and Header Bidding

Supply decisions affect yield and header bidding performance. By coordinating SPO with yield optimization and exchange settings, Ranjan ensures that both buyers and sellers see the benefits of cleaner supply paths.

Supply Path Optimization FAQs

What is supply path optimization (SPO)?
SPO is a buyer practice used to select and prioritize the best paths to inventory based on efficiency, transparency, and performance. It helps reduce wasted spend and improve campaign outcomes.
How does Ranjan Dasgupta approach SPO?
Ranjan uses data to identify redundant or low-value paths, then collaborates with partners to simplify supply without sacrificing reach. He pays close attention to how changes will appear in buyer tools and logs.
What standards are important for SPO?
Standards such as sellers.json and ads.txt/app-ads.txt are foundational. They allow buyers to verify who is authorized to sell what inventory and through which relationships, which is central to effective SPO.
How does SPO relate to yield optimization?
Cleaner supply paths often lead to better pricing and stronger buyer trust, which can support long-term yield. Ranjan links SPO work with yield optimization strategies so that publishers see the revenue impact.
